Help

RobustQ is a web service to quantify cellular robustness of metabolic models. Robustness is the ability of a biological system to uphold its functionality in spite of perturbations. M.P. Gerstl et al. (2015) were the first to describe a quantitative measure for robustness with a sound theoretical foundation, which is the backbone of this work.
We measure the counterpart of robustness by enumerating the number of minimal cut-sets (MCS) in a network and then calculating the probability of failure (PoF) of a network in respect to its objective function (e.g. biomass growth). We focus only on the k-shortest MCSs since they have the highest impact on network robustness and enable us to evaluate even genome-scale metabolic models.
You can learn more about the method here.

How to get started:

  1. Create an account or Sign In
  2. Queue up your job by uploading a valid SBML model and setting the desired parameters.
  3. Wait for the job to finish. This may take up to 24 hours, depending on the model size.
  4. It may happen that your job fails. Please check the logs for further details or try tweaking parameters.
